Foundation

What is virtue-led education?

At Nishkam School, the fostering of human virtues forms the foundation of all goodness and hence is a foundation for all education.

Our virtues-led education approach helps to provide guidance to enable pupils to understand their choices in order to help lead better lives. Our pupils become self-reflective and flourish; they are able to build strong, meaningful relationships and understand their responsibilities to the global family and all creation, founded in faith.

Virtues education is central to all of school life, whether it is in the informal curriculum, how we speak and conduct ourselves or through acts of regular service. We focus on 45 core virtues as described in the Nishkam Passport. These virtues are not abstract ideas — they are qualities we practise every day, in every lesson, in the way we treat one another and in the work we produce.

Our curricula are carefully enriched to allow experiences where our pupils, teachers and parents alike learn to grow through a conscious focus on virtues. This is what makes Nishkam different — and it is what makes your Business Studies education here more than just a qualification.

Pillar I

Faith-Inspired

Learning from the wisdom of all traditions. Businesses that act with integrity and compassion reflect values at the heart of every great faith.

Pillar II

Virtues-Led

Fostering compassion, humility, justice and courage. Every business decision is an ethical choice — a chance to practise virtue or fall short of it.

Pillar III

Aspiring for Excellence

Becoming the best human beings we can possibly be. In Business Studies, this means thinking critically, writing with precision, and achieving Grade 9.

Daily

Peacefulness, Diligence, Determination.

Our three classroom virtues, practised in every lesson, every day. These are not just words — they shape how we learn, how we behave and how we grow.

Peacefulness

In every lesson

When your teacher calls for Peacefulness, the room falls silent. You are calm, focused and ready to listen. A peaceful learner absorbs information fully, considers different perspectives with respect, and creates an environment where everyone can learn without distraction.

Diligence

In every lesson

When your teacher calls for Diligence, you sit up straight, hold your pen, eyes forward, ready to work. A diligent learner gives their full effort to every task — not just the easy ones. They check their work, refine their answers, and never submit anything less than their best.

Determination

In every lesson

Determination means pushing through when the work gets hard. It means attempting the 12-mark question you want to skip. It means treating every mistake as a stepping stone rather than a setback. A determined learner keeps going when others give up — and that is what separates good from outstanding.

In Business Studies specifically: Peacefulness helps you approach complex stakeholder conflicts and ethical dilemmas with a calm, considered mind. Diligence drives you to build complete chains of analysis rather than settling for surface-level points. Determination pushes you through the most demanding 9 and 12-mark questions, where the difference between Level 3 and Level 4 is the willingness to persist with depth and precision.
